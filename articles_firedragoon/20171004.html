Another season has come and gone and Team Solomid (TSM) finds themselves once again atop the North American League of Legends Championship Series (LCS) after another dominant domestic campaign.

\r\n\r\n

The team pegged as the great North American hope comes into Worlds hoping to finally live up to the hype.

\r\n\r\n

TSM has not had a meaningful international showing since Intel Extreme Masters (IEM) Katowice in 2015. Since then they have struggled to recapture a championship level of play.

\r\n\r\n

This year's Mid Season Invitational serves as a perfect example of TSM struggling to make it to the main event of the play-in stage and failed to make the knockout stage after that.

\r\n\r\n

There is a lot to like about TSM at Worlds this year. They are coming off of a convincing NA LCS final where they dispatched Immortals 3-1.

\r\n\r\n

They've been taking the time to focus on playing a variety of compositions instead of getting very good at a singular type of composition.

\r\n\r\n

To top it all off, at the beginning of the Summer split they welcomed back their star marksmen, Doublelift. This came after seeing Hauntzer take on a much more vocal role in the team, which resulted in him putting up a near MVP worthy performance over the course of the season.

\r\n\r\n

They appear to be head and shoulders above the rest of the very competitive NA LCS scene, but the same question still rings true for TSM; “Is it enough?”

\r\n\r\n

TSM has not made it out of the Group Stage since 2015 where they were quickly ousted from the tournament by the eventual World Champions, Samsung White (SSW). (Note: TSM gave SSW their only loss during their near flawless run to a title).

\r\n\r\n

However, the draw seems to be in the team's favor this time around. They were picked into a group with the Flash Wolves, Team WE (World Elite) and Misfits. Meaning they are in a group without any Korean teams, the less threatening of the two European finalists; World Elite, who qualified through the play-in phase; and the Flash Wolves, whom while they seem to be SK Telecom T1 (SKT)'s kryptonite, have struggled to look as dominant against other teams.

\r\n\r\n

The odds certainly seem to be in their favor when compared to their past group draws which paired them with the likes of two time world finalist Royal Never Give-up, Samsung Galaxy, KT Rolster, and Origen, who, in case you forgot, managed to reach the semifinals in 2015.

\r\n\r\n

That being said, if all goes well TSM could be in prime position to make a run at a World Championship and finally give their fans a long awaited knockout stage appearance.

\r\n\r\n
