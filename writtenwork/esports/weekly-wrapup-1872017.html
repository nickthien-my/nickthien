Weekly Wrap-Up - 18/7/2017

Keeping you up to date with what happened in FDG over the past week.
Published: 2017-7-18

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive

The team took part in the online World Cyber Arena 2017 APAC OPEN QUALIFICATION on Friday evening. In the first round of 128, the team had a bye and advanced to the next round where they would face fellow Malaysian team, Virtual Genesis Esports, in the round of 64. The team managed to beat VG, 16-10.
Later that evening in the round of 32, the team went against Vietnamese team, UTM CS:GO, one of the biggest professional esports team in Vietnam. UTM blew past the CSGO team and was eliminated from the qualifier with the final score of 8-16. The team finished in the Top 32 and is unable to qualify for the main WCA 2017 APAC event.


FIFA17

Over the weekend, the FIFA17 duo had two events. The first event was Gamesbro's FIFA17 Tournament that was held at Gamesbro on Saturday. Amirul "AmTuah" Afandi was sadly knocked out early in the top 32 by Falcon Esports representative, KaizerHaz, who was knocked out by another fellow Falcon Esports player in the next round.
Boon Shin "Shige" would see more action at Gamesbro, after the group stages. His first game was against Prodigy FIFA Gaming's Ace. Shige beats Ace, 5-3. In the round of 16, Shige would help his fellow teammate take back a win from Falcon Esports by beating their representative, Dakwos, 3-1.
Continuing his winning streak, Shige would next face Kickers' Abul. Going 1-1 in normal time, they would head into the penalty shootout with Shige coming out on top and moving on to the semifinals. Shige had hoped to go 4 for 4 against the esports companies that are competing in Gamesbro by beating Infinite Esports' Nakata. Sadly, Shige was unable to overcome the champion of the tournament. Losing to Nakata, 3-1.
After that lost, Shige was sent to the loser's final to compete for third place against Prodigy's Sora. Shige managed to defeat Sora, ultimately achieving the second runner-up position.
Their next tournament at Seven Legends Gaming Studio on Sunday, Shige was sadly knocked out early on while AmTuah went on to be the champion of the event.


Overwatch

The team had an eventful two-days at C2AGE X-treme 2017 at HELP University, Subang 2 Campus. On Saturday, the team only had one game against 5F1M and won their first match in the tournament and advanced to the winner bracket finals on Sunday.
In the winner bracket finals, the team went against SoloQ Gaming, who the team defeated last month during Midnight Gaming's Jom Main! Overwatch Tournament. So there is definitely a desire from the opposing team to overcome the team. The team managed to hold their ground and sent SoloQ to the loser's finals.
This would not be the last time that the teams meet. As SoloQ proved their tenacity by beating Malaysian Battlefield Team - Mixed Team in the loser's finals and faced off against the team one more time in the grand finals. However, SoloQ was unable to find a winning solution for the FDG team and the team beat SoloQ, 3-0, and ended the day as the champions of the tournament.
However, due to the team participating in this LAN tournament, they had to forfeit their match with Xavier Esports in the ESL Open Division due to scheduling, this drops the team down to 10th place.
